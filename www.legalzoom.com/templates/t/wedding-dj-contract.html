Wedding DJ Contract
This Wedding DJ Contract (hereinafter referred to as the Contract) is made and entered on [Effective Date] (hereinafter referred to as the Effective Date),

By and Between

[Client Name] (hereinafter referred to as the Client), residing at [Client Address], and;

[DJ Name] (hereinafter referred to as the DJ), with its principal office at [DJ Address].

The Client and the DJ are collectively referred to as the Parties and individually as the Party.

WHEREAS the Client wishes to engage the DJ to provide the disc jockey (DJ) services at their wedding (hereinafter referred to as the Event) and the DJ agrees to provide the DJ services or musical entertainment (hereinafter referred to as the Services) at the Event on the terms and conditions hereinafter set forth.
TERMS AND CONDITIONS.
1. EVENT DESCRIPTION.
The DJ agrees to provide their Services at the following venue and time:

(a) Event Venue: [Event Address]
(b) Event Date: [Event Date]
(c) Estimated Start Time: [Estimated Start Time]
(d) Estimated End Time: [Estimated End Time]
2. DJ SERVICE.
The DJ shall provide up to [Number of Hours of DJ Services] hour(s) of DJ service for the Client at the Event. The DJ service shall consist mainly of providing musical entertainment in a recorded music format.
3. PAYMENT.
In consideration of the Services provided by the DJ, the Client shall pay the sum of [Total Payment] to the DJ (hereinafter referred to as the Total Event Cost or Fee). A non-refundable security deposit in the amount of [Non-refundable Security Deposit] (hereinafter referred to as the Deposit") shall be paid by the Client to the DJ upon the signing of this Contract. This Deposit will be deducted from the Total Event Cost and will be shown in the invoice statement provided by the DJ to the Client.

If additional hours of DJ Services are requested beyond the agreed-upon time in this Contract, they will be charged at the rate of [Per hour fee for every additional hour] per hour, to be paid on the day of the Event.

If any invoice is not paid by the due date, a late fee of [Late Payment Fee] shall be charged to the Client.
4. TERM.
This Contract shall commence as of the Effective Date and terminate on [Contract Termination Date].
5. REQUIREMENTS.
DJ requires the following space and electrical requirements in order to provide DJ Services:

(a) Space Requirements. The DJ requires a space of [Dimension of the space required] area for setting up the space for equipment, speakers, and lighting stands.

(b) Electrical Requirements. The DJ requires a minimum of [Electrical Circuit Requirement in Amps] ampere circuit(s) from a reliable power source within [Distance to Power Source in Feet] feet (measured along the wall) of the set-up area. Additionally, the DJ requires access to [Number of Required Outlets] electrical outlet(s) to power the equipment.
6. DJ'S RESPONSIBILITIES.
(a) The DJ shall arrive at the venue at least [DJ Event Setup Time] hour(s) before the Event starts to set up and conduct a soundcheck.
(b) The DJ affirms that it is familiar with indoor and outdoor setups and sound mixing.
(c) The DJ shall include guest's requests in the playlist whenever possible.
(d) The DJ shall play the music non-stop unless stopped by the Client.
(e) The DJ shall use their own equipment, tools, and materials, such as a high-quality microphone, sound system, turntables, etc., to provide the aforementioned service.
7. CLIENT'S RESPONSIBILITIES.
(a) The Client shall provide the DJ with a written music planner or music request list [Music Request List_Submission Period] day(s) before the Event.

(b) The Client shall make all necessary arrangements to provide the DJ with access to the Event Venue not less than [DJ Event Setup Time] hour(s) before the Event for the setup and [DJ Event Takedown Time] hour(s) after the completion of the Event for the takedown.
8. TERMINATION.
(a) The Client may terminate this Contract upon [Termination Notice Period_Client] day(s) prior written notice to the DJ. Upon termination, the Client shall be entitled to a refund of any amount paid minus the Non-refundable Deposit.

(b) The DJ may terminate this Contract upon [Termination Notice Period_DJ] day(s) prior written notice to the Client. If the DJ terminates this Contract, a suitable DJ replacement must be provided, subject to the Clients approval. Also, upon the termination, the DJ shall refund all the amount previously paid by the Client, including the Non-refundable Deposit.
9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY.
The Client shall be liable for any and all injury or damage to the DJ or equipment of the DJ while at the Event, if the damage is caused by the Client or its guest.

In no other circumstance shall either Party be liable to the other Party or any third party for any indirect or consequential damages resulting from any part of this Contract in any way.
10. INDEMNIFICATION.
Both Parties agree to indemnify, defend, and hold the other party harmless from and against any claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys fees) arising out of or in connection with this Contract.
11. WARRANTY.
Each Party represents and warrants that they are at least 18 years of age and have the authority to enter into this Contract.

The DJ further warrants that it shall provide Services mentioned in this Contract in a timely and efficient manner and agrees to make every effort to provide satisfactory service.
12. CONFIDENTIALITY.
Both Parties hereby agree that they shall not disclose any confidential information to any third parties throughout the duration of this Contract and shall not use the confidential information for any purpose except those detailed herein or expressly authorized by the other Party.
13. ASSIGNABILITY.
Neither Party may assign this Contract or the rights and obligations thereunder to any third party without the other Party's prior express written approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.
14. NOTICES.
Any notices required or permitted by this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by certified mail or courier to the mentioned address.
15. FORCE MAJEURE.
Neither Party shall be liable for any failure in performance of the obligation under this Contract due to cause beyond that Party's reasonable control (including and not limited to any pandemic, fire, strike, act, or order of public authority, and other acts of God) during the pendency of such event.
16. MODIFICATION.
No modification of this Contract shall be made unless in writing signed by both Parties.
17. SEVERABILITY.
If any term, clause, or provision hereof is held invalid or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, all other terms will remain in full force and effect until the Contract termination.
18. ARBITRATION.
In the event of any dispute arising in and out of this Contract between the Parties, it shall be resolved by arbitration. There shall be [Number of Arbitrators] arbitrator(s) who shall be appointed by [Arbitration Appointing Party Name]. The venue of arbitration shall be [Location of Arbitration], and the Seat shall be [State of Seat]. The arbitrators' decision shall be final and binding on both Parties.
19. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION.
This Contract shall be governed following the laws of the [Governing Law]. If arbitration cannot resolve the disputes under this Contract, they shall be resolved by litigation in the courts of the [Jurisdiction], including the federal courts therein, and the Parties shall comply with the jurisdiction of such courts, agree to accept service of process by mail and hereby waive any jurisdictional or venue defenses otherwise available to them.
20. LEGAL AND BINDING CONTRACT.
This Contract is legal and binding between the Parties as stated above. The Parties each represent that they are eligible and authorized to enter into this Contract.
21. ENTIRE CONTRACT.
This Contract constitutes the entire understanding of the Parties, revokes and supersedes all prior agreements between them, and is intended as a final expression of their Contract.
ACCEPTANCE AND SIGNATURE.
The Parties acknowledge that they have read the above Contract in its entirety, understand all of its terms and conditions, and agree to abide by the terms of this Contract as demonstrated by their signatures as follows:

Wedding DJ contract: How-to guide

A wedding is a life-changing decision for both the bride and groom. The ambiance of the wedding or reception venue holds immense importance on the wedding day. Every bride and groom look forward to a lively and cheerful atmosphere to make their wedding day memorable and enjoyable for the guests. The services provided by a professional DJ set the perfect tone for the wedding. A wedding DJ plays a key role in keeping the energy high, engaging the guests, and weaving through the day's significant moments.

A wedding DJ contract is vital for both the DJ and their clients. It ensures that the DJ delivers a carefully crafted musical experience that aligns with the couple's expectations, with no scope for confusion or misunderstandings.

Follow this guide to understand a wedding DJ contract, its benefits, and the critical components.

What are DJ services?

"DJ services" cover all the aspects of delivering music for entertainment. These services can vary significantly between different acts and performers. DJs, or disc jockeys, are responsible for providing DJ services and creating an engaging experience for their audience. They provide these DJ services at various events, such as weddings, private parties, corporate events, festivals, and more.

What skills must professional DJs possess?

Professional DJs possess diverse skills that enable them to use advanced technologies and equipment to create an enjoyable experience while providing DJ services. Some of these skills include:

  • Understanding of music with a deep technical knowledge
  • Comprehending the crowd's demands and playing the most appropriate songs
  • Blending and mixing songs to create smooth sound effects
  • Engaging and communicating with the audience with a focus on their work
  • Troubleshooting technical difficulties
  • Demonstrating professionalism and courtesy to both the crowd and the client

Who hires wedding DJ services?

Event planners, wedding venue owners, and individuals such as bridesmaids, best men, or even the bride and groom can require wedding DJ services for wedding functions. 

What is a wedding DJ contract?

A wedding DJ contract is a mutually beneficial agreement between a DJ and their clients. This document allows the DJ and their clients to outline their expectations, such as:

  • How will the DJ (or disc jockey) perform the DJ services?
  • The duration for which the DJ would provide their services. 
  • The desired music or playlist the DJ must play.
  • The potential for the DJ to accommodate requests from guests.
  • How much will the DJ charge the client? 
  • The mode and time of payment.
  • What'll happen if someone cancels the contract?

In short, DJ contracts present the complete picture of the event, with details of the event description as per the client's requirements, payment terms, and the deliverables that the DJ can provide.

What are the benefits of having a DJ contract?

DJs must have a written contract for their stability and success as paid artists. For the clients availing of such services, a DJ contract ensures that the DJ performs and delivers the perfect soundtrack for each moment of the wedding. Here are the key benefits of a DJ contract:

1. Brings forth professionalism and competence

The contract helps DJs present a professional, experienced, and trustworthy image and rationalizes their pricing. 

2. Imparts comprehensiveness to the contract

This document includes all the details needed to make an event memorable. It covers the professional DJ services provided, such as playing music from different time periods, genres, or moods to suit the event's atmosphere and keep the audience engaged. 

3. Gives clarity on payment terms

A wedding DJ contract outlines the payment details, including the DJ's fees, payment schedule (if payments are made in installments), and the accepted payment methods. It also covers any required deposits and overtime rates, if applicable. Additionally, the contract specifies any extra expenses the client is responsible for, such as transportation, accommodation, meals, and more.

4. Provides coverage for cancellations/rescheduling

A wedding is a lifelong commitment between a couple, involving economic, social, and emotional aspects. It requires coordination by many people to ensure that the wedding day or reception runs smoothly. Sometimes, due to unforeseen circumstances, such a grand event may need to be canceled or rescheduled for a different date. In such cases, the client and the DJ may incur significant losses. The refund policy of such contracts protects both parties from losses due to any last-minute cancellations.

5. Includes permissible provisions

The contract includes provisions to protect the DJ and their clients from potential obstacles to hosting a seamless event. It allows the DJ to deliver high-quality services at weddings, receptions, and other events without being distracted by regulatory concerns, thus preserving the DJ's artistic freedom.

What should a wedding DJ contract include?

As a new DJ, venturing into the music industry with wedding DJ contracts can be an exciting opportunity to learn and grow. With the right mindset and a willingness to learn, this process can be a fantastic learning experience that sets the stage for a successful career in the industry. The important clauses every wedding DJ contract should include are:

1. Event description 

This section describes the details required to perform at the event, such as the event date, time, place, sound system, and required amps. 

2. Scope of service 

As a DJ, use this section to describe your services at the event, such as providing musical entertainment in a recorded music format and the number of service hours. Also, specify the services you may not be providing or which may incur extra costs.  

3. Payment 

In this section, provide the due date for the payment, the applicable late fees, and any deposits required. The contract must clarify the expectations regarding the security deposit. For instance, if it's a refundable deposit, specify how it would be accommodated in the final settlement. Be sure to include the extra charges for any additional hours you play beyond the agreed period.

4. Term

As a DJ, knowing when each contract starts and ends is essential. Including the start and end dates in the contract helps avoid double bookings and ensures the client understands the contract's timeframe.

5. Requirements

As a disc jockey has specific space and electrical requirements, it's the client's responsibility to provide them to the DJ. In this section, the DJ may list the criteria, such as space and electrical requirements, to ensure a smooth event flow.

Space requirements: The DJ's space requirements are crucial for the quality of the service and the overall event experience. The DJ needs space for the DJ platform setup, optimal speaker output, and lighting stands. 

Electrical requirements: The DJ requires a circuit outlet from a reliable power source within the setup area and a backup source. It ensures that the DJ setup operates smoothly without any power disruptions.

6. Responsibilities

This section outlines the responsibilities of each party under the contract. The DJ agrees to provide the performance and related services to ensure a successful event. The client agrees to provide appropriate access and make other necessary arrangements. A list of the responsibilities of each party may include

Responsibilities of the DJ

  1. The DJ, an expert in event setup and soundchecks, must promptly arrive at the venue at the time specified by the client or earlier to initiate the event set up and conduct the necessary soundchecks. 
  2. The DJ shall include guests' requests in the playlist whenever possible, ensuring a personalized and enjoyable experience for all attendees.
  3. The DJ shall play the music non-stop unless stopped by the client.
  4. To provide the service mentioned above, the DJ shall use their equipment, tools, and materials, such as a high-quality microphone, sound system, turntables, and more.

Responsibilities of the client

  1. The client shall provide the DJ with a written music planner or music request list a specified number of days before the event.
  2. The client shall make all necessary arrangements to ensure the DJ has access to the event venue a set number of hours before the event starts for setup and a set number of hours after it ends for windup.

7. Cancellation/termination policy 

Sometimes, unforeseen circumstances may arise that could necessitate the cancellation of a contract. To ensure a fair and transparent process, including a cancellation policy in a DJ contract is essential. This section outlines the cancellation terms, providing both parties with a sense of security and protection. 

8. Limited liability

With the best precautions in place, accidents may happen during an event. This section states each party's liability regarding any injury or damages caused to the other. 

9. Indemnification

This section states that both parties agree to protect and hold each other harmless from any claims, damages, losses, and expenses arising from or related to this contract. However, both parties might be held responsible if they intentionally cause the problem.

10. Warranty

This section confirms that both parties are at least 18 years old and have the authority to enter into this contract. The DJ also guarantees they'll promptly and efficiently provide the services outlined in this contract, ensuring they meet the client's requirements.

11. Notices

This section mentions the process to follow if the DJ is required to send notice to the client. Generally, the notice is sent with a certified mail service.

12. Governing laws

In this section, the parties agree to interpret the document according to the applicable state laws. 

13. Entire agreement

This clause of the contract represents the complete agreement between the parties involved. It invalidates and replaces any previous agreements and is meant to serve as the ultimate expression of their contract.

14. Signatures

This clause requires both the parties, i.e., the DJ and their client, to sign the contract once it is ready. A DJ and their clients can sign the contract using LegalZoom's eSignature service.

Frequently asked questions 

What should be in a DJ contract?

A DJ contract should contain the following information:

  • Event description and details, including the date, time, place, equipment, and more
  • Scope of service
  • The charges for the services with the payment terms
  • Cancellation terms in case odd situations arise
  • Placeholders for signatures

Why should you have a DJ contract for a wedding?

Here's why a wedding DJ contract is important:

  • Assures that the client has signed up the DJ for the wedding 
  • Fosters a healthy relationship between the DJ and the client, ensuring a successful and enjoyable occasion
  • Clarifies both parties' responsibilities and safeguards their interests 
  • Addresses the essential aspects of the performance in a professional manner 
  • Illustrates payment terms discreetly and clarifies the refundability aspect 
  • Incorporates the protective clauses for the DJ and their clients in detail

Should I get a DJ for my wedding?

Whether you want to have a DJ for your wedding is your call. A DJ helps set the occasion's atmosphere and fuels the grand occasion. 

How long do DJs play at weddings?

The duration of the service depends on the details of the contract signed between the contracting parties. Usually, the DJs provide their services for around 4-5 hours. 

Can I DJ at my wedding?

Yes, you can DJ your wedding, but it takes time and effort. It would help if you compiled a good playlist beforehand, have the right equipment, and set up a stage to do it yourself. But, if you want a more professional approach to the occasion and only have a little time to plan things well, you should have a DJ.
