HVAC Maintenance Contract
This HVAC Maintenance Contract (hereby referred to as Contract) is made and entered into on [Effective Date] (hereinafter referred to as Effective Date),

By and Between

[Client Company] (hereby referred to as Client), with its registered office at [Client Address], and;

[Sender Company] (hereby referred to as Contractor), with its registered office at [Sender Company Address].
The Client and the Contractor shall be collectively referred to as Parties, and individually as Party.
WHEREAS the Client wishes to engage the Contractor for repairs or service to their HVAC system as described in this Contract and the Contractor agrees to provide such services;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and commitments contained herein, as well as other goods and valuable consideration (the receipt and sufficiency of which are to be acknowledged), the Parties do hereby agree as follows:
TERMS AND CONDITIONS.
1. SERVICES.
The Contractor hereby agrees to perform the following heating, ventilation, and air conditioning services (hereinafter referred to as Services) for the Client's HVAC system: (a) Clean or replace the HVAC systems air filters; (b) Clear clogs and clean drain lines to ensure proper flow; (c) Clean evaporator coils and condensers; (d) Replace worn pulleys and belts; (e) Replace all filters; (f) Check the refrigerant charge and any possible leaks; (g) Inspect ducts for dust, mold, or debris; (h) [Other HVAC Services].
2. PAYMENT.
In consideration of the Services provided by the Contractor, the Client shall pay the sum of [Total Amount] to the Contractor (hereinafter referred to as Total Payment). A refundable deposit of [Refundable Deposit] shall be paid by the Client to the Contractor upon the signing of this Contract. The Contractor shall provide the Client a [Invoice Schedule] invoice starting on the Effective Date. The Client shall make the payment to the Contractor within [Payment Deadline in Days] day(s) from the date of invoice. In case the payments are not made in time, the Contractor may charge a late fee of [Late Payment Fee_Penalty].
3. TERM.
This Contract shall commence on the [Effective Date] and terminate on [Termination Date].
4. MAINTENANCE AND INSPECTION SCHEDULES.
(a) Daily
(i) Check for any reported complaints and troubleshoot them immediately. (ii) Monitor AC systems of the critical facilities continuously and take action to repair them immediately. (iii) General inspection of the AC system and rectification if any abnormality exists. (iv) [Other Daily Services].

(b) Monthly
(i) Clean condenser and evaporator coils.
(ii) Check and change the HVAC system's air filters.
(iii) [Other Monthly Services].

(c) Quarterly
(i) Inspect the connection for any water leaks in the coil and connection.
(ii) Check and clean the drain pan, condensate drain pipe, and floor drains to ensure no choking and flooding.
(iii) Check all types of air filters.
(iv) Check heater bank condition and repair it if any problem exists.
(v) Inspect the condition of insulation materials and correct them if required.
(vi) [Other Quarterly Services].

(d) Yearly
(i) Check all motor amperage draws.
(ii) Connect gauges and check operating pressures.
(iii) [Other Yearly Services].
5. ACCESS TO THE CLIENT'S PREMISES.
The Client accepts responsibility for providing the Contractor with sufficient access to the premises where Services are being performed on the scheduled date of work.
6. INSPECTION.
The Client reserves the right to inspect all work performed under this Contract. If the work performed by the Contractor is not in accordance with this Contract or does not match the Client's expectations, the Client may notify the Contractor in writing. At that time, the Contractor shall promptly correct such work within a reasonable time.
7. CONDITION OF PREMISES.
The Contractor accepts responsibility for keeping the premises clean and orderly and for removing all debris from the premises as needed during work hours.
8. USE OF UTILITIES.
The Client shall allow the Contractor to use the premises' water and power, at no cost, to render the Services and execute the work.
9. INSURANCE.
During the term of this Contract, the Contractor shall obtain and maintain all applicable insurance as required by any applicable law to cover its employees, property damage, or any third-party bodily injury arising from or in any way related to the Services or the site where the work is being performed. The Contractor's lack of insurance coverage shall limit any liability under this Contract.
10. RELATIONSHIP OF PARTIES.
The Client is hiring the Contractor as an independent contractor under the scope of this Contract. The Client and the Contractor shall not establish any partnership or employer-employee relationship.
11. TERMINATION.
Either Party may terminate this Contract if any Party fails to perform any of its obligations under this Contract. If such failure is not cured within [Number of days to remedy breach] day(s) (unless extended by the Party) after written notice to the Party at fault, the Parties shall be entitled to seek and obtain all remedies available to them in law.
12. CONFIDENTIALITY.
During the term, the Contractor shall not disclose any confidential information of the Client that is communicated to the Contractor in connection with the above-mentioned Services. Such information shall be held by them in strict confidence. This provision shall continue to be effective after the termination of this Contract.
13. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY.
In no event shall either Party be liable to the other Party or any third party for any damages or special, indirect, or consequential damages that might result from any part of this Contract, such as, but not limited to, loss of profit, revenue, or failure in the delivery of Services.
14. INDEMNIFICATION.
The Contractor agrees to defend, indemnify, and hold the Client harmless from and against all claims, liabilities, losses, deaths, injuries, damages, costs, and expenses suffered or incurred by the Contractor arising from the Contractor's negligence in the performance of the Contract.
15. ARBITRATION.
In the event of any dispute arising in and out of this Contract between the Parties, it shall be resolved by arbitration. There shall be [Number of Arbitrators] arbitrator(s), who shall be appointed by [Arbitration Appointing Party Name]. The venue of arbitration shall be [Location of Arbitration], and the Seat shall be [State of Seat]. The arbitrators' decision shall be final and binding on both Parties.
16. ASSIGNABILITY.
Neither Party may assign this Contract or the rights and obligations thereunder to any third party without the prior express written consent of the other Party, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.
17. SEVERABILITY.
In the event that any provision of this Contract is deemed to be invalid or unenforceable, in whole or part, that part shall be severed from the remainder of this Contract, and all other provisions shall remain in full force and effect as valid and enforceable.
18. GOVERNING LAW.
This Contract shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the [Governing Law] without regard to conflict of law principles. If the disputes under this Contract are not resolved by arbitration, the disputes shall be resolved by litigation in the courts of the [Jurisdiction], including the federal courts therein, and all the Parties consent to the jurisdiction of such courts, agree to accept service of process by mail, and hereby waive any jurisdictional or venue defenses otherwise available to it.
19. ENTIRE CONTRACT.
The Parties acknowledge that this Contract sets forth and represents the entire contract between them. If the Parties are willing to change/add/modify any terms, they shall be in writing and signed by both Parties.
20. NOTICES.
Any notice required by this Contract shall be in writing and given to the appropriate Party by personal delivery, certified mail, prepaid postage, or any such agreed-upon delivery service.
21. FORCE MAJEURE.
Neither of the Parties shall be liable for any failure in performance of any obligation under this Contract due to causes beyond that Party's reasonable control (including and not limited to any pandemic, fire, strike, act, or order of public authority and other acts of God) during the pendency of such event.

HVAC maintenance contract: How-to guide

Even a well-designed heating and cooling system needs regular maintenance to perform well and deliver the best results. As HVAC contractors, it's crucial for you to provide your customers with the best HVAC systems.

If you're in the HVAC business, a maintenance contract is important. Follow this guide to explore what an HVAC maintenance contract is, the services included in it, the different types of HVAC maintenance contracts, and the critical components of such a contract.

What is an HVAC system?

An HVAC, also known as the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system, includes a set of technologies and equipment designed to create and maintain a comfortable indoor environment.

  • HVAC systems are vital in regulating temperature, humidity, and air quality in indoor spaces, ensuring that occupants remain warm during colder seasons and cool during warmer seasons.
  • These systems have air filtration and purification components that help promote healthy indoor air quality by removing dust, allergens, and other pollutants.
  • They regulate humidity levels to create an optimal and comfortable indoor environment for occupants.

What is an HVAC maintenance contract?

An HVAC maintenance contract is an agreement between HVAC service providers and their customers, such as a residential owner or a commercial customer. It outlines the service providers' responsibilities, such as delivering regular HVAC maintenance for the property's heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems for a set fee (usually upfront). It binds both parties to carry out their duties and responsibilities.

Why is an HVAC maintenance contract required?

Setting up and maintaining an HVAC system can be quite expensive for owners and often requires specialized technical know-how. To reduce these costs, many HVAC companies offer an HVAC maintenance agreement to address customers' HVAC service requirements.

What are some other names for an HVAC maintenance contract?

An HVAC maintenance contract is known by many names, such as:

  • HVAC service contract
  • HVAC repair contract
  • Heating ventilation and air conditioning contract

What are the benefits of having an HVAC service contract?

An HVAC service contract benefits both HVAC companies and their clients. Let's explore these advantages for each of the party:

1. Revenue generation

HVAC service contracts benefit HVAC companies, as they generate revenue for service providers from seasonal maintenance visits and the sale of replacement equipment.

2. Regular maintenance

At times, property owners may miss the due date for general maintenance of the HVAC system owing to their busy work schedules and other reasons. Having an HVAC service contract ensures that regular maintenance of the asset is being done, thus improving the energy efficiency of the complete air conditioning unit. 

3. Priority service

Having an HVAC service contract in place gives the client access to priority service without any hassle, ensuring the best performance of the HVAC equipment.

4. Cost savings

Choosing a package instead of paying for individual services may lead to cost savings for the customer, making it an attractive option. Also, having a reliable HVAC contractor who can be given an emergency call to identify any issues early on can help customers avoid paying for costly repairs.

This also enhances brand reliability, and customers endorse such brands through word of mouth to their friends and relatives, thus making it beneficial for the HVAC company.

5. Discounted repairs

Clients who choose such service contracts may require services over a longer period at frequent intervals, so the contractor may offer discounted repairs, which benefits the clients.

6. Peace of mind

Knowing that professionals are taking care of your HVAC system gives property owners peace of mind. Once they are satisfied with the maintenance procedure, they might show interest in using other products of the same company.

What are the different types of HVAC maintenance contracts?

HVAC service providers may offer comprehensive maintenance plans or service contracts encompassing a range of services to ensure your HVAC equipment's optimal functioning and longevity. Some of the major types of such contracts are:

1. Full-service contract

Though this is the most expensive contract, it's an all-inclusive package that covers all the required aspects of an HVAC service plan. This includes:

  • Parts
  • Labor
  • Preventive maintenance
  • Emergency service
  • Replacement of equipment under the same pricing

2. Labor-only contract

A competitive workforce is essential for ensuring smooth operations in any technical work. As the name suggests, the labor-only contract focuses on the service technicians of HVAC service contracts.

  • Labor or service technicians required for repairs
  • Replacement services
  • Maintenance

3. Preventive maintenance contract

This contract covers maintenance for your HVAC system to ensure its optimal functioning. This includes:

  • The periodic inspection of the HVAC system, such as light repair work.
  • It also specifies the circumstances under which equipment replacement may or may not be included.
  • Such contracts require biannual and quarterly visits from HVAC service providers. 

What should an HVAC maintenance contract include?

Here are the key points to include in an HVAC maintenance contract: 

1. Tenure of the contract

The contract must state how long a client wants to hire HVAC contractors or service providers. Generally, commercial clients hire HVAC contractors for at least one year.

2. Frequency

The contract must specify how often inspections or service calls will be done, i.e., the frequency with which the client requires your routine maintenance service, such as weekly, monthly, or yearly.

3. Business hours

Mention the business hours when the inspection or maintenance services will be provided.

4. Details of the equipment

The contract must mention all equipment that requires maintenance, its brand, model, age, serial numbers, and location. It should also specify the warranty provided on replacement equipment.

5. Maintenance services

This section of the contract describes the different maintenance plans and service contracts provided by the HVAC companies, which in some cases, may be available as customizable packages. However, the main aspects include the following:

  • Oversight
  • Parts replacement
  • Maintenance schedule
  • Service-call priority to the client

6. Pricing

The contract should detail the pricing of the replacement equipment and how much you will charge the client for the services offered.

7. Payment options

The HVAC service contract should stipulate the payment options, whether an HVAC service provider receives payment at every visit or bills its clients quarterly or annually. The mode of payment should also be included.

8. Permissions

Here, the contract outlines the permissions the contractor should have to provide maintenance services. The contract should explicitly mention that HVAC company service technicians can access the HVAC owner's property with access to the required utilities, such as water and electricity, to execute their work properly. Subsequently, the contractor can agree to not cause any property damage, and leave the premises clean by removing debris or any residual elements left after the work.

9. Inspection

This section states the client's right to inspect the work carried out by the service provider under the contract. It mentions that the client may write to the service provider if the work doesn't meet the contract's requirements or expectations. The service provider must make the necessary corrections within a reasonable timeframe.

10. Nature of the relationship

This part explains the contractor's relationship with the client. It states that the contractor is not the client's employee or partner.

11. Termination

This section outlines the specific circumstances that would result in the termination of the agreement, such as either party's failure to perform any of its obligations, which can be informed to the other party with a written notice. It also mentions the duration in which the parties must rectify the gap caused by the failure, after which they can seek legal assistance.

12. Limited liability

This section explains each party's responsibility for any injury or damages caused to the other. For example, if the service provider fails to deliver the specified services, it may cause damage to the client's system. On the other hand, if the client doesn't pay the specified charges on time or denies access to the required facilities, it may result in revenue loss for the service provider.

13. Indemnification

This section describes a contractor's responsibility for protecting the client from any claims, damages, losses, and expenses if such an incident occurs due to the contractor's negligence. It helps ensure the client's best interests under the contract.

14. Notice

This section mentions the process to follow if a written notice has been sent to either party and the mode of notice delivery. 

15. Governing law

In this section, the involved parties agree to interpret the document according to the relevant state laws.

16. Signatures

This is where both parties, the HVAC company and its client, sign the contract once it is ready. 

17. Entire agreement

This clause of the contract signifies the complete agreement between the parties involved. Any changes, such as adding or deleting terms, shall be written and signed by both parties.

Why should you have an HVAC maintenance checklist?

Keeping an HVAC maintenance checklist is a good practice as:

  • It helps HVAC businesses efficiently conduct inspections and maintenance of HVAC systems.
  • It assists them in quickly identifying the signs of HVAC failure.
  • It helps to address potential problems.
  • It ensures that HVAC systems are in proper condition.

What's included in an HVAC maintenance checklist?

To ensure your client's cooling and heating systems are performing at their best, it's vital to conduct check-ups. As HVAC service contractors, it's best to have a checklist ready to check the respective parts of the HVAC system. Here's what an ideal HVAC maintenance checklist for air conditioning systems must include:

  1. Replace condenser and evaporator coils
  2. Change air filters and clean the unit
  3. Clean drain lines to ensure proper flow
  4. Check the air conditioner for any possible refrigerant leaks
  5. Tighten electrical connections
  6. Inspect ducts for mold, dust, and debris
  7. Test compressors, start capacitors, and relays
  8. Check fan motor
  9. Check blowers and blades for proper airflow and clean them if required
  10. Inspect the air conditioner's condensate drain
  11. Check for debris around the outside unit
  12. Lubricate moving parts
  13. Replace filters on heating equipment
  14. Examine the ignition burner assembly
  15. Inspect the heater's fuel lines for faulty gas or oil connections
  16. Inspect heat exchanger or heating elements
  17. Inspect for gas leaks with gas furnaces
  18. Check gas pressure
  19. Clean the blower fan and air handler
  20. Check thermostats and controls
  21. Lubricate bearings and motors and any other moving parts
  22. Replace belts and pulleys
  23. Clear drain lines and pans of standing water to prevent overflows
  24. Check the heat pump
  25. Check the warranty of significant parts
