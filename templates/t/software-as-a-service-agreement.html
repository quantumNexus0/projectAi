By and Between 
[Customer Name] (hereinafter referred to as “Customer”), incorporated at [Customer Address], and; 
[Sender Company] (hereinafter referred to as “Company”), incorporated at [Sender Company Address].
The Company and the Customer shall hereinafter collectively be referred to as “Parties,” and individually as “Party.”
The Company and the Customer agree that the following terms and conditions will apply to the Services provided under this Agreement and Orders placed thereunder.
TERMS OF THE AGREEMENT.
1. DEFINITIONS.
(a) “Customer Content” means all relevant data, including all text, sound, video or image files, and software, that are provided by the Customer to the Company for the SaaS Services. (b) “Documentation” means the written and/or electronic release notes, user guides, online help, training materials, and/or other published technical documentation about the applicable Service provided by the Company to the Customer, together with access to the Service. (c) “Order” means any ordering document between the Customer and the Company that specifies the Service being purchased. (d) “Software” means the object code version of any software to which the Customer has been provided access as part of the Service, including all updates or new versions. (e) “Services” refers to the Company's internet-accessible (including all other technical and non-technical services) service made available by access to and use of software products hosted by the Company to which the Customer has subscribed under the relevant Order, including any documentation, updates, upgrades, support, and content. (f) “Subscription Term” shall mean the period specified in this Agreement during which the Customer shall have access to Software for use and operation through the Company’s SaaS Services. The Subscription Term shall renew for successive [Renewal_Time Period] unless either party delivers written notice of non-renewal to the other Party at least [Non-Renewal Notice Period_No. of Days] day(s) prior to the expiration of the then-current Subscription Term.
2. LICENSE GRANT.
(a) During the Subscription Term, the Customer shall receive a non-assignable, non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide right to access and use the SaaS Services solely for their internal business operations under the terms of this Agreement.

(b) The Customer shall acknowledge that this Agreement is a services agreement, and the Company shall not deliver copies of the Software to the Customer as part of the Services.
3. CUSTOMER RESPONSIBILITIES.
(a) In connection with its use of the Services, the Customer shall:
(i) comply with all applicable laws; (ii) comply with any codes of conduct, policies, or other notices provided by the Company; (iii) immediately notify the Company if the Customer becomes aware of a security breach or unauthorized access related to the Service. (b) In using the Services, the Customer shall not: (i) reproduce, post, or distribute any code, software, or other material protected by copyright, privacy rights, or any other intellectual property right without first obtaining written consent from the Company; (ii) provide false identity information to gain access to or use the Service;
(iii) reverse engineer, disassemble, decompile, or otherwise derive the source code of the Software for the Services, except and only to the extent such activity is expressly permitted by applicable law; (iv) access the Services or use the Documentation in order to build a similar or competitive product. (c) License from Customer: Subject to the terms of this Agreement, the Customer shall grant to the Company a limited, non-exclusive, and non-transferable license to reproduce, modify, and display Customer Content solely as deemed necessary to provide the Services to the Customer.
4. OWNERSHIP AND RESTRICTIONS.
(a) The Customer retains the intellectual property rights and ownership in and to its Customer Content. (b) The Company or its licensors retain all ownership and intellectual property rights to the Software, Services, Documentation, and anything developed, improved, modified, and/or delivered under the Agreement. (c) Third-party technology that may be appropriate or necessary for use with the Company's programs is specified in the Program Documentation or ordering document, as applicable. The Customer’s right to use such third-party technology is governed by the terms of the third-party technology license agreement specified by the Company and not under this Agreement.
5. SERVICE DESCRIPTION.
The Company agrees to provide the Customer with access to [Name of Software Service] (“Service”) as a hosted, web-based solution. The Service includes the provision of the following:
(a) Core Functionality. The Service includes [Service Inclusions].
(b) Support and Maintenance. The Company will provide technical support, updates, and maintenance as per the format agreed upon by the Parties.
(c) Service Availability. The Service will be available all the time unless there is scheduled maintenance, which shall be communicated by the Company to the Customer accordingly in writing.
(d) Data Storage and Backup. The Service Provider will ensure secure storage and regular backup of Customer data as per the terms detailed in the Company's data management policy. Such data management policy shall be made available to the Customer.
(e) Limitations and Exclusions. The Service does not include [Service Limitations]. Any additional services required by the Customer will be subject to separate terms and charges. Any other service description not provided for under this Agreement shall be separately agreed to by the Parties in writing as an annexure or supplement to this Agreement.

[Service Fee]
The above Fee is based on [Factors affecting the service fee] and is invoiced every [Frequency of invoice]. Either party may terminate this SaaS Agreement immediately upon a material breach by the other Party that has not been cured within [No. of days to remedy material breach] day(s) after receipt of notice of such breach.
(c) Suspension for Non-payment. The Company may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect by delivering notice of the termination to the Customer if the Customer fails to pay the invoice amount on time, but only after the Company notifies the Customer of such failure, and such failure continues for [Termination Notice Period_Non-Payment] day(s).
(d) Effect of Termination.
(i) The Customer shall immediately pay to the Company all amounts outstanding as of the date of termination and any amounts outstanding as a result of termination. (ii) The Customer shall cease all use of the Service upon the effective date of the termination. Software as a Service agreement: How-to guide

A SaaS agreement is an integral part of any company providing SaaS services. A Software as a Service (SaaS) agreement or SaaS contract is created when a software service provider agrees to offer their services to a customer. This agreement defines:

  • The terms and conditions regarding the end user's control and access to the software
  • The subscription services
  • The payment model to the customers
  • The restrictions on the use of product/service by the service provider
  • Steps to be taken and the course of action in the event of a data breach
  • Legal obligations as a user
  • Termination policy upon any dispute

SaaS agreements include legal terms, and drafting an all-inclusive agreement may require technical expertise. In this guide, we will discuss the important components of SaaS agreements, and the benefits of having an agreement template to draft a SaaS agreement.

What are the advantages of having SaaS agreements?

A SaaS agreement details the obligations and performance goals of involved parties. A well-structured SaaS agreement is beneficial for a software company as it assists all parties in finding common ground in various matters. Here are some advantages:

  • Presenting legal matters in a professional manner
  • Protecting the rights of the company (as well as customers involved in the SaaS agreement)
  • Accelerating the negotiation process
  • Identifying potential issues
  • Outlining resolutions clearly    

What are the essential components of a SaaS agreement?

While SaaS agreements vary according to your business and the services provided, the following components are crucial to any SaaS agreement. As a SaaS agreement includes legal and regulatory considerations, please consult an attorney for the proper assistance to ensure your Software as a Service (SaaS) agreement complies with local and state regulations.

1. Introduction

In this part of the agreement, briefly introduce your company, customer details, respective addresses, date, and subscription plan details. 

2. Terms of the agreement

This section describes the guidelines on how the end user should use the services. Here are the different customer and service provider obligations: 

3. Definitions 

Some end-users may need assistance to comprehend a SaaS agreement. To simplify things, it's recommended to explain the definitions of the terminologies used in the agreement. For instance, you may define the terms like “Schedule,” “Subscription Term,” “Order,” etc.

4. License grant

In this section, mention the license rights of the end users for the services provided as per the agreement.

5. Customer responsibilities

This section lists the responsibilities of the customer. For instance, customers should agree to the applicable law, follow the usage guidelines, and adhere to the stipulation mentioned in the service license.

6. Ownership and restrictions

This section describes how the customer should handle intellectual property rights and manage confidential information. It also mentions each party's ownership and restrictions on the services.

7. Orders and payments

In this section, inform the users about the payments. This may include:

  • The amount the customers have to pay for the ordered services, including a payment schedule and the accepted mode of payment.
  • It also addresses the frequency of the billing cycle as per the SaaS subscription. Generally, SaaS companies offer monthly, annual, semi-annual, multi-year billings, or one-time subscription options.
  • The fees for the SaaS product's maintenance, upgrades, and other support services.

8. Term and termination

Sometimes, an agreement can end before the services have been completed. This section specifies the conditions under which the contract remains valid and when it can be mutually terminated.

9. Service level agreement

SaaS service-level agreements, or SLAs, define the services provided by a company and how its customers should utilize or access them. In this section, mention the terms and conditions for hosting the SaaS software, the support and maintenance services required, and any exclusions for which the SaaS provider should not be obligated.

10. Warranties

A warranty is a promise or guarantee made by the service provider about the performance or quality of the software services provided. In this section, mention the warranty and clearly define your responsibility as a service provider.

11. Limitations of liability

A limitation of liability agreement shields SaaS providers from risk by contractually prohibiting end users/customers from holding them responsible for specific damages. In this section, mention the extent of damages for which you may be accountable as a SaaS vendor under the agreement. 

12. Indemnification

Indemnification is a party's promise not to hold another party legally accountable for possible breaches or damages in an agreement. This section details what kind of breach or loss the service contract does not cover.

13. Confidentiality

This section states the software company's surety to keep the customer's data, trade secrets, confidential information, and other personal data private and not divulge any details to any third parties. The software service provider also acknowledges that the customer/client information will only be utilized for business needs. The customer also promises to retain all the outlined company-related service data and other confidential information within their business, even after the termination of the SaaS agreement.   

14. Arbitration

Arbitration is like an out-of-court dispute resolution where an impartial third party, known as an arbitrator, hears evidence and provides a ruling. This section states that any disputes between the parties resulting from or connected to the SaaS contracts are resolved by this provision.

15. Notice

This section mentions how a written notice should be sent to either party.

16. Force majeure

This section releases a party from its obligations if its performance is made impossible by an event beyond its control despite its best efforts (e.g., fire, flood, earthquake, war, and more).

17. Governing law

In this section, the involved parties agree to interpret the document according to the relevant state laws.

18. Entire agreement

This clause of the contract signifies the complete agreement between the parties involved. Any changes, such as adding or deleting terms, shall be written and signed by both parties.

19. Acceptance and signatures

This section establishes that the parties involved in the SaaS contract agree with the conditions mentioned in the document and readily accept and sign it.

Frequently asked questions

What is a Software as a Service agreement?

A Software as a Service (SaaS) agreement is an agreement between a SaaS provider and its customers. In this document, a company specifies the terms and conditions to be followed by the end user. The agreement also provides guidelines on managing subscriptions and payments. 

What is a SaaS service level agreement?

SaaS service-level agreements (SLAs) define the services a software company provides and how its customers should utilize or access them. They may include the terms and conditions for hosting the SaaS product, support, maintenance, intellectual property rights (IPR), and ownership rights. 

What is the difference between a SaaS and a license agreement?

There are many differences between SaaS and license agreements. However, the most important structural difference is the delivery model.

A SaaS agreement is software accessible on a hosted platform in an intangible form where the data is stored and protected by the SaaS provider. 

License agreement software is stored within the end user's system or available in a physical form such as CD-Roms, hard disks, or DVDs.

What should I look for in a SaaS agreement?

In a SaaS agreement, you should look for the software's usage, billing and payment details, data privacy and confidentiality clauses, intellectual property rights and ownership, and renewal and termination policies.
